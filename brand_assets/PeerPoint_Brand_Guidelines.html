Visual Identity

Logo Usage

The PeerPoint Capital logo consists of a circular chart icon with an upward-trending arrow, paired with the bold wordmark. Always use approved vector files — never recreate.

Primary Logo — Full Color
Use on white or light backgrounds (#F5F7FA or lighter). Default for all brand applications.
Reversed Logo — White Knockout
Use on Navy (#1B3A6B) or Dark Navy (#0D2240) only. Never on white or light backgrounds.
Alternate Full-Color Variant
For documents, presentations, and co-branded materials on light gray backgrounds.
CLEAR ZONE = height of “P”
Clear Zone (Exclusion Zone)
Minimum clear space equal to the “P” height on all sides. No text or graphics may enter.

Minimum Sizes

ApplicationMinimum (Digital)Minimum (Print)
Website header160px wide—
Social profile imageIcon mark 180×180px—
Business card—1.25 in (31.75mm)
Letterhead—2.0 in (50.8mm)

Logo Do’s & Don’ts

✓ DO
✗ DON’T
Use full-color logo on white or light backgrounds
Stretch, compress, rotate, or skew the logo
Use the white logo on navy/dark backgrounds
Recreate the logo in a different font or layout
Maintain the required clear zone at all times
Apply drop shadows, glows, or 3D effects
Scale proportionally using corner handles only
Use the logo in any unapproved color
Use vector files (SVG/AI/EPS) for all print use
Place logo on visually busy or clashing backgrounds

Visual Identity

Imagery & Photography

PeerPoint imagery should feel real, optimistic, and purposeful — reinforcing that capital creates opportunity. Authentic over staged; warm over clinical.

🏪
Business Owners
Small business owners in real environments — restaurants, storefronts, workshops. Candid, not posed.
🌆
Urban Environments
Chicago and US metro areas. Diverse, community-focused, and representative of real working America.
📈
Growth Moments
Expansion milestones, new openings, team wins. Forward momentum is always the visual subject.
✓ DO
✗ DON’T
Natural, warm lighting with golden undertones
Cold, harsh, or flat studio lighting
Candid, action-oriented shots of real work
Overly staged stock (handshakes on white bg)
30–40% breathing room for text overlays
Piles of cash, coins, or clichéd financial imagery
High-res: 300 DPI print / 72+ DPI digital
Heavily filtered or oversaturated images

Application

Digital Guidelines

Digital applications must be consistent, accessible, and mobile-first. All platforms use Inter and the core brand color system with WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility compliance.

🌐
Website
  • Font: Inter, 16px min body size
  • Max content width: 1200px
  • Primary CTA: Navy bg, white text
  • Secondary CTA: Green bg, white text
  • Links: Accent Blue #2563A8
  • WCAG 2.1 AA compliant always
  • Mobile-first responsive design
📱
Social Media
  • Profile: Icon mark 400×400px
  • LinkedIn cover: 1584×396px
  • Facebook cover: 820×312px
  • Twitter/X cover: 1500×500px
  • Post images: 1200×630px
  • Hashtags: 3–5 per post max
  • Tone: Slightly conversational
📧
Email Marketing
  • Max template width: 600px
  • Header logo: 200px wide
  • Headline: 24–28px Navy
  • Body: Inter 16px, 1.6 line-height
  • CTA: Navy or Green button
  • Footer: Navy bg, white text
  • Unsubscribe link required

Partnerships

Co-Branding Rules

When PeerPoint Capital appears alongside partner logos, the brand must retain its visual integrity and hierarchy. All co-branded materials require marketing approval before use.

Partnership TypeLogo Rule
Co-primary partnershipBoth logos equal size, separated by 1px gray rule, on shared background color
PeerPoint as primary sponsorPeerPoint logo 25% larger; PeerPoint positioned left or top
PeerPoint as secondary sponsorPartner logo leads; PeerPoint secondary on right or below
"In partnership with" treatmentDescriptor in Calibri 10pt italic above/below the secondary logo
✓ DO
✗ DON’T
Place both logos on the same background color
Lock logos together with no clear separation
Maintain individual clear zones for both logos
Place PeerPoint logo on partner brand colors
Use official partner logo files only
Allow partners to use our logo without written approval

Brand Protection

Brand Don’ts

Every team member and external partner is responsible for following these rules. When in doubt, contact the marketing team before using the brand.

Logo
✗Stretch, compress, rotate, or skew the logo
✗Recreate the logo in a different font or layout
✗Use the logo in any unapproved color
✗Add drop shadows, glows, outlines, or 3D effects
✗Place the full-color logo on a dark background
✗Place the white logo on a white or light background
✗Violate the clear zone surrounding the logo
✗Display the logo below approved minimum sizes
Color & Typography
✗Use colors outside the approved brand palette
✗Use pure black #000000 for body text (use #4A4A4A)
✗Apply gradients to the logo or primary brand elements
✗Use decorative, script, or novelty display fonts
✗Set body copy in all capitals
✗Mix more than two typeface families in one design
✗Use color combos that fail WCAG 2.1 AA contrast
✗Use Green as a dominant background in formal docs
Messaging & Digital
✗Promise specific approval outcomes in marketing
✗Use financial jargon without plain-language explanation
✗Use JPEG/PNG screenshots of logo for print
✗Publish off-brand posts from company accounts
✗Allow third-party use of brand without written approval
✗Publish external comms without marketing team review
